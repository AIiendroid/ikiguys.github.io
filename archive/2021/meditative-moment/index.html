We meditated on a compass of cold stones, calling out to the forces of East and West.

A simple bench, made of four bricks and a rectangle of unknown rock with a leopard's scattering of small stones. I nestled with my back to wise brother Stone, stretching my heels to the sun. The palm trees rustle, I pray: "Give us, great dragon, equal love and kindness to all around, unconditional and unselective. For the breeze that has swooped does not know that my body is not one with the stone, that those who hold hands are taken to be counted as 2. For I am both Dina and Dasha and Maha.
