Huanchaco ‐ Sleepy surf town

Huanchaco ghost town

Huanchaco is a sleepy city with a frozen time that spreads through the streets with a fog. Our first point on the map after Lima: the city is squeezed between the ocean and the desert, three or four big streets, the highest building ‐ church on a hill.

A picture of Huanchaco formed in our heads from reading a couple of articles about the place: dashing surfers catching waves of the Pacific Ocean, something always going on around and music flying with the wind, but we're apparently four years too late (most of these blogs date back to 2017), because now the surfers became ghosts of the beach, dissolving into the waves. The half-empty city is shrouded in salty fog and gives the impression that the colonisers just arrived on a distant continent and trying to survive in the desert, hastily building houses and cafes. So let's update 2017 by jumping to May 2022 and talk about Huanchaco today.

Huanchaco in 2022

Because of the pandemic, the once rather touristy place is deserted, but that makes the city even more appealing. You can't expect warm sunny weather every day in Huanchaco, as strange as it may seem for the desert, it is quite cool, so we occasionally strolled through the streets wrapped in sleeping bags.

Prices

The promenade is dotted with hostels and restaurants, where the prices are 2-3 times higher than average, so if you're traveling on a tight budget, it's better to walk a little inside the city, and ask the locals where you can sleep and eat at an adequate price. The local cafes have a Menu option, this implies a fixed price for which you get a small entry, a second (main meal) and a drink. A good price for such a pleasure is between 8 and 15 Soles. You can find room for 20-30 Soles per person per night, in expensive hotels the price goes up to 90-120 Soles.

Surfing

For me, beginner who is just learning to get up on a board, Huanchaco is a very good place. It takes 2-3 minutes and some effort to get to the break. There is plenty of time between waves to have rest and just enjoy the embrace of the ocean. The average wave height is 0.5-1 meter, but many waves are mountainous rolling over the surface without breaking. A little more effort and patience, and you're near the third break, where the waves are much higher and you can stay on such a wave for about a minute.

There are enough places in the city where you can rent a board + wetsuit for 40-50 Soles for the whole day.

How to get there from Trujillo?

You can get here by a red bus with a brightly colored Huanchaco sign, it will cost 2.50 Soles. Wherever you are, ask the locals Huanchaco bus and a friendly stranger will point you in the right direction and reward you with a smile. The road goes through the slums, but then you see the ocean on the horizon and the desert gives way to the coast.

The town itself is very pleasant, it's calm and peaceful. Locals buy fresh bread and newspapers in the morning, and sporadic silhouettes glide through the early waves. Huanchaco is a great place for slow meditations with the sound of the ocean, lazy surfing and spending time with yourself, away from the noise of the big city. Grab a sweater, it's not as hot as you might expect!
